What do I eat that keeps me feeling so great?

Food + Supplements

My diet is centered heavily around vegetables, fruits, nuts, oils, lentils, seeds, and grains.

  1. Breakfast

    2. For breakfast I sometimes have whole grain toast with flaxseeds, sunflower feeds, and oats, with a vegan, non-dairy butter and strawberry preserves. I might also make a cup of vegan ramen with chopped up green onions and garlic for added anti-inflammatory benefits and fiber.

  2. Lunch

    3. For lunch I usually have a salad with spinach leaves, broccoli, cucumber, parsley, more garlic, carrots, and a vegan caesar dressing, topped off with walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseeds, and hemp seeds, all of which are rich in anti-oxidents, protein, healthy fats, and omega-3 fatty acids.

  3. Dinner

    4. For dinner I might try something more savory like a soup or chili with lentils, kidney beans, pinto beans, black beans, tomatoes, kale, and a vegan meat substitute. The other day I made homemade margarita pizza with fresh roma tomatoes, basil leaves, olive oil, salt and pepper,and a non-dairy vegan cheese. My wife is on a gluten-free diet, so I made her pizza dough with a gluten-free almond flour.

To ensure that I have a healthy, balanced diet, I use a combination of plant-based foods and vitamin supplements so that my body gets all the nutrition it needs. Each morning, I take a regimen of vitamin supplements, including:

  • Omega-3
  • Vitamin D
  • Calcium
  • Tumeric (to reduce inflammation)
  • Vitamin B12
  • Probiotic
  • Multivitamin

For omega-3, it is important to get the 3 major varieties of omega-3 fatty acids: ALA, EPA, and DHA. While your body naturally converts ALA into EPA and DHA, it takes a lot of ALA to convert enough EPA and DHA to meet your body's needs. Since EPA and DHA come primarily from fish, vegans can take omega-3 from an algae alternative that contains EPA and DHA while sparing the lives of fish and avoiding the mercury and other contaminants found in fish. For my ALA, I also take a flaxseed omega-3 oil supplement. For more information on omega-3s, click here.

B12 is not so much a vitamin as it is a microorganism that we consume. In the past, B12 microorganisms were found naturally growing in soil and plants and both humans and animals got enough B12 by consuming both the plants and animals. However, with the heavy use of pesticides and other chemicals, we are not getting enough B12 from eating plants, and farmers have to feed B12 supplements to their livestock to ensure that the animals are getting enough B12. As a result, the safest bet for both vegans and non-vegans is to take a B12 supplement to ensure that you are getting enough of this vital nutrient.
